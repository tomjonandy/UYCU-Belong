‘We are each one on a road going towards home...’ Home is often where we feel we fit in, where we can truly express who we are. Some of us might find it in a physical place, others in a family or within a certain friendship group.

No matter who you are, what you believe or where you come from: we all love to belong.

Join us as we explore God’s invitation to belong during a week of exciting events. Free lunches, a coffeehouse and great evenings of entertainment, scroll down to find out more...

LUNCHES

A chance to get stuck into the Big Questions many people have about where Christianity does or does not belong, accompanied by a delicious free lunch. Afterwards, there is a chance to have more conversation and ask further questions.

12pm - 1pm, in the Belong Marquee

Monday 12:00

Myths and Fairy tales: Does the bible belong on the fiction shelf?

The fundamental basis of the Christian faith - a book written over thousand of years by various authors and translators. Can it be considered reliable or does it belong on the fiction shelf?

Tuesday 12:00

Crusades and Terrorism: Does conflict belong in Christianity?

Our world history is defined by decades of violence and war, many of which were rooted in conflicts over religious thoughts and ideas. Can Christianity, or religion for that matter, truly be good when it arguably encourages conflict?

Wednesday 12:00

Disease and Disasters: Can a suffering world belong to a God of Love?

How many times in a month, week or even a day do we receive news of natural disasters and wars or grieve over our own personal suffering? If God, supposedly one of unconditional love, created this world, why does He allow us to struggle through pain?

Thursday 12:00

Sex and Clubbing: Can fun belong in Christianity?

Work hard, play hard. That’s what university is all about, right? Getting your degree while enjoying sex and alcohol and having the time of your life. Can Christians really get all the fun of the university experience, while a strict God has them playing by the rules?

Friday 12:00

Fact or Fiction: Does the Resurrection belong in mythology?

Robin Hood, King Midas, Pandora’s Box and...the Resurrection? The story of Jesus Christ rising from the dead is surely just another interesting story that is ultimately untrue - a myth. There can’t really be enough sound historical evidence to support such an event, can there?

EVENINGS

Those February evenings always bring a bit of a chill but don’t worry, the Belong Marquee has heating! Not just that, but it will also be home to nights of entertainment, including acoustic music, comedy and a magic show. We will also be looking through the book of John, one of the four gospels of the new testament and we will explore the life and person of Jesus Christ in the form of short talks. What better way to spend your mid-term evenings?

8pm - 10pm, in the Belong Marquee

Monday 12:00

Personality and Purpose

It’s hard to know what our purpose is in life and which personality set we belong to. Join us as we enjoy acoustic music and look at the personality and purpose of Jesus Christ.

Featuring: acoustic artist, Nathanael Orr, and student drama performances

Tuesday 12:00

Free from fear

Come along to discover the journey of a man whose life was defined by violence but following a prison sentence, found where he truly belonged.

Featuring: an interview with ex-UVF fighter, David Hamilton

Wednesday 12:00

Worried about worth

A night of students entertaining other students. Jazz, coffee and a short talk on social anxieties, a major issue as we search for a place to belong.

Featuring: Student Jazz Band, and student drama performances

Thursday 12:00

Laughing through life

Oh, what a treat is Thursday night! We have the hilarious Tony Vino joining us and a short talk on how Jesus can be great fun. Come along, it’ll be a barrel of laughs!

Featuring: professional comedian, Tony Vino

Friday 12:00

The Resurrection: Relevant?

Abracadabra! That’s right, our finale is a magic show performed by the illusionist, Todd Alexander - his tricks are sure to raise the roof. Afterwards, we will look at the Resurrection. If it is true, so what?

Featuring: illusionist, Todd Alexander
