LUNCHES

A chance to get stuck into the Big Questions many people have about where Christianity does or does not belong, accompanied by a delicious free lunch. Afterwards, there is a chance to have more conversation and ask further questions.

12pm - 1pm, in the Belong Marquee

Monday 12:00

Myths and Fairy tales: Does the bible belong on the fiction shelf?

The fundamental basis of the Christian faith - a book written over thousand of years by various authors and translators. Can it be considered reliable or does it belong on the fiction shelf?

Tuesday 12:00

Crusades and Terrorism: Is Religion Harmful?

Our world history is defined by decades of violence and war, many of which were rooted in conflicts over religious thoughts and ideas. Can Christianity, or religion for that matter, truly be good when it arguably encourages conflict?

Wednesday 12:00

Disease and Disasters: Can a suffering world belong to a God of Love?

How many times in a month, week or even a day do we receive news of natural disasters and wars or grieve over our own personal suffering? If God, supposedly one of unconditional love, created this world, why does He allow us to struggle through pain?

Thursday 12:00

Sex and Clubbing: Can fun belong in Christianity?

Work hard, play hard. That’s what university is all about, right? Getting your degree while enjoying sex and alcohol and having the time of your life. Can Christians really get all the fun of the university experience, while a strict God has them playing by the rules?

Friday 12:00

Fact or Fiction: Does the Resurrection belong in mythology?

Robin Hood, King Midas, Pandora’s Box and...the Resurrection? The story of Jesus Christ rising from the dead is surely just another interesting story that is ultimately untrue - a myth. There can’t really be enough sound historical evidence to support such an event, can there?
